George Brown College of Applied Arts and Technology is a public, fully accredited college of applied arts and technology.  
It was chartered in 1966 and has three campuses in downtown Toronto.  
George Brown offers many different programs in art and design, business, community services, early childhood education, construction and engineering technologies, health sciences, hospitality and culinary arts, preparatory studies, as well as specialized programs and services for recent immigrants and international students.  
It is located on the traditional territory of the Mississaugas of the Credit First Nation and other Indigenous peoples who have lived here over time. The college is named after George Brown, 19th century politican and the founder of 'Toronto Globe'.
